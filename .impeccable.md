## Design Context

### Users

Quill Watermark serves photographers exporting polished portfolio images and creators producing social cover assets. They use the app in focused editing sessions where the output image is the priority and metadata or brand framing is adjusted as part of final presentation, not as a secondary admin task.

### Brand Personality

The product should feel editorial, precise, and composed. It should evoke the atmosphere of a late-night editorial desk: serious enough for professional image finishing, but still visually refined and content-led rather than technical for its own sake.

### Aesthetic Direction

The approved direction is an editorial production desk with dark mode as the primary experience and a fully supported light theme for bright working environments. The visual language should borrow the discipline of a premium image tool workspace, but translate it into a magazine-like layout system instead of a camera-brand dashboard.

Typography should pair `Noto Serif` for titles and display moments with `Noto Sans` for controls, labels, field values, and dense UI copy. Accent color use should be sparse and deliberate, centered on a saturated yellow that marks primary actions, active states, and key toggles.

### Design Principles

1. Keep the artwork at the center of attention; the interface exists to frame and refine the output.
2. Treat the workspace like an editorial desk: structured, dense, and calm rather than decorative or playful.
3. Use contrast through typography, spacing, and panel hierarchy instead of heavy card chrome.
4. Preserve a professional dual-theme system: dark for immersive editing, light for proofing and review.
5. Make every control feel production-ready, with clear states and restrained accent usage.
